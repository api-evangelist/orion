Digital health solutions often assume users have reliable devices, stable internet, app navigation skills, and health literacy, yet availability is not the same as accessibility. As digital tools become the primary healthcare entry point, design choices significantly impact who can easily access care. The article examines women's health as a case study, noting that existing healthcare inequities can be replicated in digital environments when underlying research gaps persist.
