Every health system pays an interoperability tax. It is not always visible on a balance sheet, which is part of the problem. It appears as repeated imaging, duplicated pathology, delayed referrals, incomplete medication histories, manual workarounds, and technology teams spending scarce resources integrating around legacy decisions.
