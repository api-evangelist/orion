The article argues that clinical capacity, not funding or technology, is healthcare's primary constraint, with insufficient clinicians driving reduced access, higher costs, burnout, and lower productivity. McKinsey Health Institute research suggests addressing the global healthcare workforce shortage could generate $1.1 trillion in economic value, avert 189 million years of life lost, and reduce global disease burden by 7%. It urges treating clinician shortages as a strategic board-level issue, focusing on retention, productivity, and care model redesign alongside workforce expansion.
